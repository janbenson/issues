                                    7     SEC. 3. FINDINGS; SENSE OF THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTA-
                                    8                             TIVES REGARDING DIABETES PREVENTION
                                    9                             UNDER THE MEDICAID PROGRAM.

                                  10               (a) FINDINGS.—Congress makes the following find-
                                  11 ings:
                                  12                         (1) The prevalence and cost of diabetes is a sig-
                                  13               nificant concern for State Medicaid programs. By
                                  14               2021, the Medicaid program is expected to cover
                                  15               13,000,000                people           with         diabetes            and    about
                                  16               9,000,000 people who may have pre-diabetes. By
                                  17               2021,              States            will         spend              an         estimated
                                  18               $83,000,000,000 on individuals with diabetes or pre-
                                  19               diabetes.
                                  20                         (2) The National Diabetes Prevention Program,
                                  21               as established by the Centers for Disease Control
                                  22               and Prevention, has been proven to reduce the onset
                                  23               of diabetes in at-risk adults by 58 percent, using a
                                  24               cost-effective, community-based intervention.
