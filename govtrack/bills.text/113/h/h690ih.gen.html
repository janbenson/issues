H. R. 690

To amend title 10, United States Code, to modify the per-fiscal year calculation of days of certain active duty or active service used to reduce the minimum age at which a member of a reserve component of the uniformed services may retire for non-regular service.

IN THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES

February 14, 2013

Mr. LATHAM (for himself and Mr. WALZ) introduced the following bill; which was referred to the Committee on Armed Services

A BILL

To amend title 10, United States Code, to modify the per-fiscal year calculation of days of certain active duty or active service used to reduce the minimum age at which a member of a reserve component of the uniformed services may retire for non-regular service.

    Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives of the United States of America in Congress assembled,

SECTION 1. SHORT TITLE.

    This Act may be cited as the ‘Reserve Retirement Deployment Credit Correction Act’.

SEC. 2. MODIFICATION OF PER-FISCAL YEAR CALCULATION OF DAYS OF CERTAIN ACTIVE DUTY OR ACTIVE SERVICE TO REDUCE ELIGIBILITY AGE FOR RETIREMENT FOR NON-REGULAR SERVICE.

    (a) Accumulation of 90-Day Periods of Service Within Any Two Consecutive Fiscal Years- Section 12731(f)(2)(A) of title 10, United States Code, is amended by striking ‘in any fiscal year’ and inserting ‘in any two consecutive fiscal years’.

    (b) Retroactive Effective Date- The amendment made by subsection (a) shall take effect as of January 28, 2008, and as if included in the National Defense Authorization Act for Fiscal Year 2008 (Public Law 110-181) as enacted.
