113th CONGRESS

1st Session

H. R. 1037

To amend the Public Health Service Act to include occupational therapists as behavioral and mental health professionals for purposes of the National Health Service Corps.

IN THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES

March 7, 2013

Mr. TONKO (for himself, Ms. BROWN of Florida, and Ms. SLAUGHTER) introduced the following bill; which was referred to the Committee on Energy and Commerce

A BILL

To amend the Public Health Service Act to include occupational therapists as behavioral and mental health professionals for purposes of the National Health Service Corps.

    Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives of the United States of America in Congress assembled,

SECTION 1. SHORT TITLE.

    This Act may be cited as the ‘Occupational Therapy in Mental Health Act’.

SEC. 2. INCLUDING OCCUPATIONAL THERAPISTS AS BEHAVIORAL AND MENTAL HEALTH PROFESSIONALS FOR PURPOSES OF THE NATIONAL HEALTH SERVICE CORPS.

    Section 331(a)(1)(E)(i) of the Public Health Service Act (42 U.S.C. 254d(a)(1)(E)(i)) is amended by inserting ‘occupational therapists,’ after ‘psychiatric nurse specialists,’.
