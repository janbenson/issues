                                    1     SEC. 2. FINDINGS AND DECLARATION OF POLICY.

                                    2              (a) FINDINGS.—Congress finds the following:
                                    3                        (1) The production of concrete masonry prod-
                                    4              ucts plays a significant role in the economy of the
                                    5              United States.
                                    6                        (2) Concrete masonry products are produced by
                                    7              hundreds of concrete manufacturers and utilized
                                    8              throughout the United States and foreign countries.
                                    9                        (3) Concrete masonry products move in the
                                  10               channels of interstate and foreign commerce, and
                                  11               concrete masonry products that do not move in such
                                  12               channels of commerce directly burden or affect inter-
                                  13               state commerce of concrete masonry products.
                                  14                         (4) The concrete masonry products industry
                                  15               employs thousands of workers and positively impacts
                                  16               economic stability throughout the United States.
                                  17                         (5) The maintenance and expansion of existing
                                  18               markets for concrete masonry products and the de-
                                  19               velopment of new markets is vital to the welfare of
                                  20               concrete manufacturers in the United States and
                                  21               those concerned with marketing and using concrete
                                  22               masonry products as well as the general economic
                                  23               welfare of the United States.
                                  24                         (6) The concrete masonry products industry




                                  25               plays a vital role in providing safe, environmentally
                                              •S 429 IS

                                                                                             3
                                    1              sustainable, and economical construction for citizens
                                    2              of the United States and abroad.
                                    3                        (7) Concrete masonry products are used for the
                                    4              construction of structures that protect and provide
                                    5              shelter for citizens during disasters (earthquakes,
                                    6              floods, hurricanes, tornados, fire, et cetera).
                                    7                        (8) Concrete masonry structures provide energy
                                    8              efficiencies to reduce American energy demands.
                                    9                        (9) Concrete masonry provides for durable
                                  10               structures that support sustainability objectives, re-
                                  11               ducing long-term demands on natural resources.
                                  12                         (10) Concrete masonry products are made
                                  13               using locally available resources throughout the
                                  14               United States, reducing transportation and infra-
                                  15               structure demands.
                                  16                         (11) Concrete masonry products are used to
                                  17               support the construction of durable and cost-effi-
                                  18               cient, low-income housing.
                                  19               (b) PURPOSE.—The purpose of this Act is to author-
                                  20 ize the establishment of an orderly program for devel-
                                  21 oping, financing, and carrying out an effective, contin-
                                  22 uous, and coordinated program of research, education, and
                                  23 promotion, including funds for marketing and market re-




                                  24 search activities, that is designed to—

                                    7                        (3) promote the use of concrete masonry prod-

                                  14      SEC. 3. DEFINITIONS.
                                  15               For the purposes of this Act:
                                  16               (a) BLOCK MACHINE.—The term ‘‘block machine’’
                                  17 means a piece of equipment that utilizes vibration and
                                  18 compaction to form concrete masonry products.
                                  19               (b) BOARD.—The term ‘‘Board’’ means the Concrete
                                  20 Masonry Products Board established under section 5.
                                  21               (c) CAVITY.—The term ‘‘cavity’’ means the open
                                  22 space in the mold of a block machine capable of forming
                                  23 a single concrete masonry unit having nominal plan di-




                                  24 mensions of 8 inches by 16 inches.
                                              •S 429 IS

                                                                                             5
                                    1              (d) COMMERCE.—The term ‘‘commerce’’ includes
                                    2 interstate, foreign, and intrastate commerce.
                                    3              (e) CONCRETE MASONRY PRODUCTS.—The term
                                    4 ‘‘concrete masonry products’’ refers to a broader class of
                                    5 products, including concrete masonry units as well as
                                    6 hardscape products such as concrete pavers and segmental
                                    7 retaining wall units, manufactured on a block machine
                                    8 using dry-cast concrete.
                                    9              (f) CONCRETE MASONRY UNIT.—The term ‘‘concrete
                                  10 masonry unit’’ means a concrete masonry product that is
                                  11 a manmade masonry unit having an actual width of 3
                                  12 inches or greater and manufactured from dry-cast con-
                                  13 crete using a block machine. Such term includes concrete
                                  14 block and related concrete units used in masonry applica-
                                  15 tions.
                                  16               (g) DEPARTMENT.—The term ‘‘Department’’ means
                                  17 the Department of Commerce.
                                  18               (h) DRY-CAST CONCRETE.—The term ‘‘dry-cast con-
                                  19 crete’’ means a composite material that is composed essen-
                                  20 tially of aggregates embedded in a binding medium com-
                                  21 posed of a mixture of cementitious materials (including
                                  22 hydraulic cement, pozzolans, or other cementitious mate-
                                  23 rials) and water of such a consistency to maintain its




                                  24 shape after forming in a block machine.

                                    8 stitutional projects using concrete masonry products and
                                    9 to generally enhance the image of concrete masonry prod-
