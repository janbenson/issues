                                    7              Congress finds the following:
                                    8                        (1) It is in the mutual interest of the United




                                    9              States and least-developed countries to promote sta-

                                    1              ble and sustainable economic growth and develop-

                                    6                        (3) A country that is open to trade may in-

                                  11               challenge in meeting the economic requirements of
                                  12               and competitiveness necessary for globalization and
                                  13               international markets.
                                  14                         (6) The United States has recognized the bene-
                                  15               fits that international trade provides to least-devel-
                                  16               oped countries by enacting the Generalized System
                                  17               of Preferences under title V of the Trade Act of
                                  18               1974 (19 U.S.C. 2461 et seq.) and trade benefits for
                                  19               developing countries in the Caribbean, Andean, and
                                  20               sub-Saharan African regions of the world.
                                  21                         (7) Enhanced trade with least-developed Mus-
                                  22               lim countries, including Afghanistan and Ban-
                                  23               gladesh, is consistent with other United States ob-




                                  24               jectives of encouraging a strong private sector and
                                  25               individual economic empowerment in those countries.
