                                    9     SEC. 3. FINDINGS.
                                  10               Congress finds the following:
                                  11                         (1) The application of numerous and significant
                                  12               variations in State law and the implementation of
                                  13               the Patient Protection and Affordable Care Act im-
                                  14               pacts the ability of insurers to offer, and individuals
                                  15               to obtain, affordable individual health insurance cov-
                                  16               erage, thereby impeding commerce in individual
                                  17               health insurance coverage.
                                  18                         (2) Mandates for health care coverage estab-
                                  19               lished by title I of the Patient Protection and Af-
                                  20               fordable Care Act will significantly elevate health in-
                                  21               surance costs beyond State and Federal ability to
                                  22               pay.
                                  23                         (3) Individual health insurance coverage is in-
                                  24               creasingly offered through the Internet, other elec-




                                  25               tronic means, and by mail, all of which are inher-
                                  26               ently part of interstate commerce.
                                              •HR 762 IH

                                                                                              3
                                    1                         (4) In response to these issues, it is appropriate
                                    2              to encourage increased efficiency in the offering of
                                    3              individual health insurance coverage through a col-
                                    4              laborative approach by the States in regulating this
                                    5              coverage.
                                    6                         (5) The establishment of risk-retention groups
                                    7              has provided a successful model for the sale of insur-
                                    8              ance across State lines, as the acts establishing
                                    9              those groups allow insurance to be sold in multiple
                                  10               States but regulated by a single State.
                                  11      SEC. 4. REPEAL OF TITLE I OF PPACA.
                                  12               Effective as of the enactment of the Patient Protec-
                                  13 tion and Affordable Care Act (Public Law 111–148), title
                                  14 I of such Act is repealed (and any amendments to such
                                  15 title, or to amendments made by such title, made by the
                                  16 Health Care and Education Reconciliation Act of 2010
                                  17 (Public Law 111–152) are repealed), and the provisions
                                  18 of law amended or repealed by such title (or amendments)
                                  19 are restored or revived as if such title (and amendments)
                                  20 had not been enacted.
